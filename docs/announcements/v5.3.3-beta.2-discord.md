## 🧵 Follow-up thread 1 — Division of labor with AirAccount

```
**How SuperPaymaster + AirAccount split the work**

A common question: "doesn't AirAccount already do session payments / spending limits / signed authorizations?" — Yes, and that's by design.

• **AirAccount (account layer)** = WHO can sign & WITH what limits — WebAuthn passkey, Session Keys (target / selector / velocity / quota), social recovery, agent delegation.
• **SuperPaymaster (settlement layer)** = WHO pays gas & HOW it settles — gasless sponsorship, xPNTs credit/debt, reputation pricing, x402 + channel settlement.

So SuperPaymaster does NOT re-implement spending-limit or session logic — AirAccount enforces it at the account, SuperPaymaster sponsors & settles. Together they're a full stack; neither duplicates the other.
```
